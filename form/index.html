Rockefeller Center

Rockefeller Center is a large office center in New York, which was built in Manhattan's Midtown in the 1930s with money from the Rockefeller family. It was named after John Davison Rockefeller Jr. The most famous are 14 high-rise buildings with Art Deco decoration. Here are the headquarters of various corporations and the largest auction house in terms of profitability, Christie's. In 1989, the Rockefeller Center was bought from the Rockefellers by the Japanese group of companies Mitsubishi. It has been a US National Historic Landmark since 1987.
